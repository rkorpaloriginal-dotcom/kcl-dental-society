## Design Context

### Users
Prospective and current King's College London Dental Society members — dental students at KCL — browsing on mobile and desktop to check events, find committee contacts, learn about sponsors, or join. Secondary audience: sponsors/partners evaluating the society's credibility, and a future student committee who will maintain the site with no dev background.

### Brand Personality
Premium, editorial, community-driven. Three words: **premium, communal, credible**. Tagline: "Community · Careers · Culture." The site should feel like a polished student-society magazine, not a corporate SaaS product or a generic Lovable scaffold.

### Aesthetic Direction
Premium editorial/magazine style built on the current site's existing navy/gold identity (carried forward, not replaced):
- Primary/navy `#1B2A56` — dominant brand color (header, footer, hero overlay)
- Accent/gold `#C9992E` — single accent for CTAs, links, highlighted heading words (no third competing accent)
- Display type: serif (Playfair-style) headings paired with the crest logo
- Body type: sans-serif, ≥4.5:1 contrast (e.g. `#444`, never light gray)
- Real photography throughout (events, sponsors, committee); one consistent treatment for committee headshots — never a mix of real photos and placeholder template cards
- Motion: subtle scroll-reveal storytelling as progressive enhancement only — content must render in the DOM immediately, animation layers on top, never gates visibility
- Light mode (daytime browsing context — students checking events/joining, not a late-night dark-mode use case)

Anti-references: the previous Lovable-scaffolded site (dead buttons, empty pages, leftover scaffold branding, inconsistent committee imagery, carousel-hidden content, native video controls).

### Design Principles
1. No dead links/empty pages — every button/link resolves to a real destination.
2. Content-first rendering — animation is progressive enhancement, never a visibility gate (this was a specific bug on the old site).
3. Consistency over variety — one visual treatment per content type (committee cards, sponsor tiles), never mixed treatments.
4. Single accent discipline — gold is the only accent color; don't introduce competing accents.
5. Accessible by default — body text contrast ≥4.5:1, alt text on all images, 375px/768px/desktop responsive checks are part of "done."
